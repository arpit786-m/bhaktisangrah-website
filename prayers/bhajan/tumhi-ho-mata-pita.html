Tumhi Ho Mata Pita Tumhi Ho

तुम्हीं हो माता पिता तुम्हीं हो

The Universal Prayer of Surrender

📚 Bhajan 🪤 God (Paramatma) 🌐 Hindi + English

'Tumhi Ho Mata Pita Tumhi Ho' (तुम्हीं हो माता पिता तुम्हीं हो) is the Hindi poetic adaptation of the famous Sanskrit shloka 'Twameva Mata Cha Pita Twameva'. It is a supreme expression of complete emotional surrender (Sharanagati) to the Divine, recognizing God as the only true relation — mother, father, friend, and companion.

Tumhi Ho Mata Pita Tumhi Ho — तुम्हीं हो माता पिता तुम्हीं हो

तुम्हीं हो माता, पिता तुम्हीं हो। तुम्हीं हो बंधु, सखा तुम्हीं हो॥ तुम्हीं हो माता, पिता तुम्हीं हो। तुम्हीं हो बंधु, सखा तुम्हीं हो॥

⋯ ॐ ⋯

तुम्हीं हो साथी, तुम्हीं सहारे। कोई ना अपना सिवा तुम्हारे॥ तुम्हीं हो नैया, तुम्हीं खेवैया। तुम्हीं हो बंधु, सखा तुम्हीं हो॥

⋯ ॐ ⋯

जो खिल सके ना वो फूल हम हैं। तुम्हारे चरणों की धूल हम हैं॥ दया की दृष्टि सदा ही रखना। तुम्हीं हो बंधु, सखा तुम्हीं हो॥

⋯ ॐ ⋯

Tumheen Ho Maataa, Pitaa Tumheen Ho. Tumheen Ho Bndhu, Sakhaa Tumheen Ho.. Tumheen Ho Maataa, Pitaa Tumheen Ho. Tumheen Ho Bndhu, Sakhaa Tumheen Ho..

Tumheen Ho Saathee, Tumheen Sahaare. Koee Naa Apanaa Sivaa Tumhaare.. Tumheen Ho Naiyaa, Tumheen Khevaiyaa. Tumheen Ho Bndhu, Sakhaa Tumheen Ho..

Jo Khil Sake Naa Vo Phool Ham Hain. Tumhaare Charanon Kee Dhool Ham Hain.. Dayaa Kee Drishti Sadaa Hee Rakhanaa. Tumheen Ho Bndhu, Sakhaa Tumheen Ho..

🌸 Significance
This prayer strips away all worldly attachments and titles, acknowledging that every earthly relationship is temporary. True refuge is found only at the lotus feet of the Supreme Lord, who is the ultimate protector and guide.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Sanskrit original of this bhajan?

The original Sanskrit verse is: 'Twameva mata cha pita twameva, Twameva bandhushcha sakha twameva, Twameva vidya dravinam twameva, Twameva sarvam mama deva deva.'

What does the bhajan signify?

It signifies ultimate surrender (Prapatti or Sharanagati). The devotee declares to God that amidst all the uncertainties of the world, God is their only true mother, father, friend, and savior (Kheviya - boatman).
