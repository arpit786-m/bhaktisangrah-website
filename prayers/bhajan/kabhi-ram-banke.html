Kabhi Ram Banke Kabhi Shyam Banke

कभी राम बनके कभी श्याम बनके

Beautiful Bhajan on the Avatars of Lord Vishnu

📚 Bhajan 🪤 Lord Rama / Krishna 🌐 Hindi + English

'Kabhi Ram Banke Kabhi Shyam Banke' (कभी राम बनके कभी श्याम बनके) is a joyous and deeply devotional bhajan that celebrates the various incarnations (Avatars) of Lord Vishnu. The devotee lovingly invites the Lord to manifest in their life, whether in the steady, righteous form of Lord Ram or the playful, enchanting form of Lord Krishna.

Kabhi Ram Banke Kabhi Shyam Banke — कभी राम बनके कभी श्याम बनके

कभी राम बनके, कभी श्याम बनके। चले आना प्रभु जी चले आना॥ कभी राम बनके, कभी श्याम बनके। चले आना प्रभु जी चले आना॥

🌸 Significance
This bhajan emphasizes the Vedantic truth of 'Hari Anant Hari Katha Ananta' — God is one, though He takes many forms to protect the righteous. The devotee says: 'Come in any form you like, but please come!'

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the central theme of Kabhi Ram Banke?

The core theme is 'Bhakti' (Devotion) and the Hindu concept of 'Avatara'. The singer is expressing that God is one supreme energy, and they welcome the divine into their heart regardless of the specific form (Ram, Krishna, Shiva, or Vishnu).

When is this bhajan typically sung?

It is typically sung during Jagrans, Satsangs, Janmashtami, Ram Navami, and community kirtans because of its simple, engaging, and joyful rhythm that everyone can clap and sing along to.
