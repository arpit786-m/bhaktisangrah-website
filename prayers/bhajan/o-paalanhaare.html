O Paalanhaare

ओ पालनहारे, निर्गुण और न्यारे

Soulful Plea to the Almighty from Lagaan

📚 Bhajan 🪤 Lord Krishna 🌐 Hindi + English

'O Paalanhaare' (ओ पालनहारे, निर्गुण और न्यारे) is a deeply emotional and monumental bhajan from the Oscar-nominated 2001 film Lagaan. Composed by A.R. Rahman with lyrics by Javed Akhtar and sung by Lata Mangeshkar and Udit Narayan, it represents the absolute surrender of villagers facing an impossible crisis.

O Paalanhaare — ओ पालनहारे, निर्गुण और न्यारे

ओ पालनहारे, निर्गुण और न्यारे। तुम्हरे बिन हमरा कौनो नाहीं॥ हमरी उलझन सुलझाओ भगवन। तुम्हरे बिन हमरा कौनो नाहीं॥

⋯ ॐ ⋯

तुम्ही हमका हो सम्हाले, तुम्ही हमरे रखवाले। तुम्हरे बिन हमरा कौनो नाहीं॥ ओ पालनहारे, निर्गुण और न्यारे। तुम्हरे बिन हमरा कौनो नाहीं॥

⋯ ॐ ⋯

चंदा में उड़ने की शक्ति कहाँ है। सूरज के पास तो अपना ही तेज है॥ ये सब तुम्हारी ही माया है भगवन। तुम्हरे बिन हमरा कौनो नाहीं॥ ओ पालनहारे, निर्गुण और न्यारे...॥

⋯ ॐ ⋯

O Paalanahaare, Nirgun Aur Nyaare. Tumhare Bin Hamaraa Kauno Naaheen.. Hamaree Ulajhan Sulajhaao Bhagavana. Tumhare Bin Hamaraa Kauno Naaheen..

Tumhee Hamakaa Ho Samhaale, Tumhee Hamare Rakhavaale. Tumhare Bin Hamaraa Kauno Naaheen.. O Paalanahaare, Nirgun Aur Nyaare. Tumhare Bin Hamaraa Kauno Naaheen..

Chndaa Men Udne Kee Shakti Kahaan Hai. Sooraj Ke Paas To Apanaa Hee Tej Hai.. Ye Sab Tumhaaree Hee Maayaa Hai Bhagavana. Tumhare Bin Hamaraa Kauno Naaheen.. O Paalanahaare, Nirgun Aur Nyaare.....

🌸 Significance
When all human efforts seem useless, devotees turn to the 'Paalanhaare' (the Sustainer). The song refers to God as 'Nirgun' (beyond attributes) and 'Nyaare' (unique/beyond comprehension), declaring that without God's help, humanity is completely helpless.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who composed O Paalanhaare?

The music was composed by the legendary A.R. Rahman for the 2001 sports-drama epic 'Lagaan'. The soul-stirring lyrics were written by Javed Akhtar.

What does 'Nirgun aur Nyaare' mean?

Nirgun means 'formless or without physical attributes' (often used in Vedanta), and Nyaare means 'unique, distinct, or beyond comprehension'. It addresses God as the ultimate, unimaginable cosmic truth.
