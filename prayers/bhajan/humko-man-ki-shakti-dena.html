Humko Man Ki Shakti Dena

हमको मन की शक्ति देना

Classic Vani Jairam School Prayer

📚 Bhajan 🪤 God (Paramatma) 🌐 Hindi + English

'Humko Man Ki Shakti Dena' (हमको मन की शक्ति देना) is another iconic Hindi cinematic prayer that became a staple in school assemblies across India. Sung by Vani Jairam for the 1971 movie Guddi, it focuses on the greatest victory of all — victory over one's own mind and ego.

Humko Man Ki Shakti Dena — हमको मन की शक्ति देना

हमको मन की शक्ति देना, मन विजय करें। दूसरों की जय से पहले, खुद को जय करें॥ हमको मन की शक्ति देना...॥

⋯ ॐ ⋯

भेदभाव अपने दिल से साफ़ कर सकें। दोस्तों से भूल हो तो माफ़ कर सकें॥ झूठ से बचे रहें, सच का दम भरें। दूसरों की जय से पहले, खुद को जय करें॥

⋯ ॐ ⋯

मुश्किलें पड़ें तो हम पे इतना कर्म कर। साथ दें तो धर्म का, चलें तो धर्म पर॥ खुद पे हौसला रहे, बदी से ना डरें। दूसरों की जय से पहले, खुद को जय करें॥ हमको मन की शक्ति देना...॥

⋯ ॐ ⋯

Hamako Man Kee Shakti Denaa, Man Vijay Karen. Doosaron Kee Jay Se Pahale, Khud Ko Jay Karen.. Hamako Man Kee Shakti Denaa.....

Bhedabhaav Apane Dil Se Saaph Kar Saken. Doston Se Bhool Ho To Maaph Kar Saken.. Jhooth Se Bache Rahen, Sach Kaa Dam Bharen. Doosaron Kee Jay Se Pahale, Khud Ko Jay Karen..

Mushkilen Paden To Ham Pe Itanaa Karm Kara. Saath Den To Dharm Kaa, Chalen To Dharm Para.. Khud Pe Hausalaa Rahe, Badee Se Naa Daren. Doosaron Kee Jay Se Pahale, Khud Ko Jay Karen.. Hamako Man Kee Shakti Denaa.....

🌸 Significance
The prayer teaches that before conquering the world or others, one must conquer oneself ('doosro ki jay se pehle, khud ko jay karein'). It prays for emotional resilience, the ability to discern right from wrong, and the strength to forgive.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who sang Humko Man Ki Shakti Dena?

The legendary singer Vani Jairam sang this prayer for the 1971 Hrishikesh Mukherjee film 'Guddi'. The lyrics were written by Gulzar and the music was composed by Vasant Desai.

What is the meaning of 'Doosro ki jay se pehle, khud ko jay karein'?

It means 'Before seeking victory over others [the world], let us seek victory over ourselves [our mind, ego, and weaknesses].'
