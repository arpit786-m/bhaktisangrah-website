Vishnu Mantra

विष्णु मंत्र

Om Namo Narayanaya — Vishnu Sahasranama Stotram Intro

📚 Mantra 🪤 Lord Vishnu 🌐 Hindi + English

Vishnu Mantra (विष्णु मंत्र) invokes Lord Vishnu — the preserver of the universe and one of the Hindu Trinity (Brahma-Vishnu-Mahesh). 'Om Namo Narayanaya' is the Ashtakshara (8-syllable) mantra of Vishnu. Chanting on Thursdays and Ekadashi is most auspicious.

Shantakaram Bhujagashayanam Padmanabham Suresham. Vishwadharam Gaganasadrisham Meghavarnam Shubhangam. Lakshmikantam Kamalayanam Yogibhir Dhyanagamyam. Vande Vishnum Bhavabhayaharam Sarvalokaikanatham.

Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya (12-syllable mantra of Vishnu)

🌸 Significance
Lord Vishnu is the sustainer of all creation. His mantras bring protection, peace, removal of all sins, and ultimately liberation (Vaikuntha — Vishnu's divine abode).

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Om Namo Narayanaya?

'Om Namo Narayanaya' is the Ashtakshara (8-syllable) mantra of Lord Vishnu. It means 'I bow to Lord Narayana.' It is one of the most sacred mantras in Vaishnavism.

What is Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya?

This is the 12-syllable (Dwadashakshara) mantra of Lord Vishnu. 'Vasudeva' is another name of Krishna/Vishnu. This mantra, found in the Bhagavata Purana, grants liberation.

When to chant Vishnu Mantra?

Thursday (Guruvar) and Ekadashi (11th lunar day) are the most auspicious times. Vishnu mantras are also chanted during Diwali, Janmashtami, and in the morning during Brahma Muhurta.
