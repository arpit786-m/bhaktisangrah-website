Saraswati Vandana

सरस्वती वंदना (या कुन्देन्दु)

Ya Kundendu Tusharahara Dhavala

📚 Stuti 🪤 Goddess Saraswati 🌐 Hindi + English

Saraswati Vandana (सरस्वती वंदना) — beginning with the famous verses 'Ya Kundendu Tusharahara Dhavala' — is the primary prayer to Goddess Saraswati, the deity of knowledge, music, arts, wisdom, and learning. This beautiful Sanskrit hymn is chanted by students, scholars, and artists before beginning their studies or performances.

Saraswati Vandana — सरस्वती वंदना (या कुन्देन्दु)

या कुन्देन्दुतुषारहारधवला या शुभ्रवस्त्रावृता, या वीणावरदण्डमण्डितकरा या श्वेतपद्मासना। या ब्रह्माच्युत शंकरप्रभृतिभिर्देवैः सदा वन्दिता, सा मां पातु सरस्वती भगवती निःशेषजाड्यापहा॥

⋯ ॐ ⋯

शुक्लां ब्रह्मविचार सार परमामाद्यां जगद्व्यापिनीं, वीणा-पुस्तक-धारिणीमभयदां जाड्यान्धकारापहाम्। हस्ते स्फाटिकमालिकां विदधतीं पद्मासने संस्थिताम्, वन्दे तां परमेश्वरीं भगवतीं बुद्धिप्रदां शारदाम्॥

⋯ ॐ ⋯

Yaa Kundendutushaarahaaradhavalaa Yaa Shubhravastraavritaa, Yaa Veenaavaradandamanditakaraa Yaa Shvetapadmaasanaa. Yaa Brahmaachyut Shnkaraprabhritibhirdevaih Sadaa Vanditaa, Saa Maan Paatu Sarasvatee Bhagavatee Nihsheshajaadyaapahaa..

Shuklaan Brahmavichaar Saar Paramaamaadyaan Jagadvyaapineen, Veenaa-pustaka-dhaarineemabhayadaan Jaadyaandhakaaraapahaam. Haste Sphaatikamaalikaan Vidadhateen Padmaasane Snsthitaam, Vande Taan Parameshvareen Bhagavateen Buddhipradaan Shaaradaam..

🌸 Significance
Reciting the Saraswati Vandana dispels ignorance, sharpens the intellect, and invokes the blessings of the Goddess for academic success, creative brilliance, and profound wisdom.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 'Ya Kundendu' mean?

'Ya Kundendu Tusharahara Dhavala' describes Goddess Saraswati as the one who is as pure and white as the jasmine flower, the moon, and the snow; who wears white garments and holds the divine Veena.

Who chants this Vandana?

It is traditionally chanted by students before starting their daily studies, by musicians before a performance, and by devotees on Vasant Panchami to seek knowledge and remove the darkness of ignorance (Jadya).
