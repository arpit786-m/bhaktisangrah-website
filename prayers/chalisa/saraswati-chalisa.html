Saraswati Chalisa

सरस्वती चालीसा

Full Lyrics in Hindi with Meaning — For Students

📚 Chalisa 🪤 Goddess Saraswati 🌐 Hindi + English

Saraswati Chalisa (सरस्वती चालीसा) is a 40-verse prayer to Goddess Saraswati — the goddess of knowledge, wisdom, arts, and learning. Recited on Basant Panchami and before examinations, this chalisa invokes Maa Saraswati's blessings for intelligence, creativity, and success in studies.

Saraswati Chalisa — सरस्वती चालीसा

जय सरस्वती माता मैया जय जय सरस्वती माता। सद्गुण वैभव शालिनी त्रिभुवन विख्याता॥

⋯ ॐ ⋯

चंद्रवदनी पद्मासिनी द्युति मंगलकारी। सोहें शुभ हंस सवारी अति सुखदायी॥

⋯ ॐ ⋯

वीणावादिनी रागिनी ज्ञान प्रकाशिनी। कल्याणमयी कल्याणी विद्या दायिनी॥

⋯ ॐ ⋯

मधुर-मधुर मधुर बाणी बोलकर वीणा। वेद पुराण सब ग्रंथ कर दिए अधीना॥

⋯ ॐ ⋯

शुक्लाम्बरधरां देवीं, शुक्लपुष्पोपशोभिताम्। शुक्लां वीणाधरां देवीं नमस्ये वाग्देवताम्॥

⋯ ॐ ⋯

जो जन यह चालीसा पढ़े भक्ति भाव धरि। विद्या बुद्धि धन पावे जग से भय दूर करि॥

⋯ ॐ ⋯

Jai Saraswati Mata, Maiya jai jai Saraswati Mata. Sadguna vaibhav shalini, tribhuvan vikhyata.

Chandravadani Padmasini, Dyuti Mangalkari. Sohe shubh hans savari, Ati sukhdayi.

Veena vadini ragini, Gyan prakashini. Kalyaanmayee Kalyaani, Vidya dayini.

🌸 Significance
Goddess Saraswati grants knowledge, eloquence, and artistic talent. Students who recite her Chalisa gain sharp intellect, memory, and success in education. She removes ignorance and fills the mind with wisdom.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Saraswati Chalisa?

Saraswati Chalisa is a 40-verse prayer to Goddess Saraswati, who governs knowledge, wisdom, arts, music, and learning. It is especially recited by students and artists.

When to recite Saraswati Chalisa?

Saraswati Chalisa is most auspicious on Basant Panchami, Saraswati Puja, and before exams. It should be recited every morning for best results.

Can students recite Saraswati Chalisa?

Yes! Saraswati Chalisa is especially recommended for students. Regular recitation is believed to sharpen memory, improve concentration, and grant success in studies and competitive exams.
