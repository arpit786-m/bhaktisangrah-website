दोहा — Doha

Lyrics in Hindi

जय गणेश गिरिजा सुवन, मंगल मूल सुजान।
कहत अयोध्यादास तुम, देहु अभय वरदान॥

Victory to Lord Ganesha, son of Goddess Parvati, the root of joy and supreme wisdom.
Thus prays Ayodhya Das, asking you to grant the boon of fearlessness.

— ॥ —
चौपाई — Chaupai

Lyrics in Hindi

जय गिरिजाय पति दीनदयाला। सदा करत सन्तन प्रतिपाला॥
भाल चन्द्रमा सोहत नीके। कानन कुण्डल नागफनी के॥

अंग गौर शिर गंग बहाये। मुण्डमाल तन छार लगाये॥
वस्त्र खाल बाघम्बर सोहे। छबि को देख नाग मुनि मोहे॥

मैना मातु की ह्वै दुलारी। बाम अंग सोहत छबि न्यारी॥
कर त्रिशूल सोहत छबि भारी। करत सदा शत्रुन क्षयकारी॥

नन्दि गणेश सोहै तहँ कैसे। सागर मध्य कमल हैं जैसे॥
कार्तिक श्याम और गणराऊ। या छबि कौ कहि जात न काऊ॥

देवन जबहीं जाय पुकारा। तब ही दुख प्रभु आप निवारा॥
किया उपद्रव तारक भारी। देवन सब मिलि तुमहिं जुहारी॥

तुरत षडानन आप पठायउ। लवनिमेष महँ मारि गिरायउ॥
आप जलंधर असुर संहारा। सुयश तुम्हार विदित संसारा॥

त्रिपुरासुर सन युद्ध मचाई। सबहिं कृपा कर लीन बचाई॥
किया तपहिं भागीरथ भारी। पुरब प्रतिज्ञा तासु पुरारी॥

दानिन महं तुम सम कोउ नाहीं। सेवक स्तुति करत सदाहीं॥
वेद नाम महिमा तव गाई। अकथ अनादि भेद नहिं पाई॥

प्रगटे उदधि मंथन में ज्वाला। जरे सुरासुर भये विहाला॥
कीन्ह दया तहँ करी सहाई। नीलकण्ठ तब नाम कहाई॥

पूजन रामचंद्र जब कीन्हा। जीत के लंक विभीषण दीन्हा॥
सहस कमल में हो रहे धारी। कीन्ह परीक्षा तबहिं पुरारी॥

एक कमल प्रभु राखेउ जोई। कमल नयन पूजन चहं सोई॥
कठिन भक्ति देखी प्रभु शंकर। भये प्रसन्न दिए इच्छित वर॥

जय जय जय अनंत अविनाशी। करत कृपा सब के घटवासी॥
दुष्ट सकल नित मोहि सतावै। भ्रमत रहे मोहि चैन न आवै॥

त्राहि त्राहि मैं नाथ पुकारो। नहिं महाप्रभु काज हमारो॥
लै त्रिशूल शत्रुन को मारो। संकट से मोहि आन उबारो॥

मातु पिता भ्राता सब कोई। संकट में पूछत नहिं कोई॥
स्वामी एक है आस तुम्हारी। आय हरहु अब संकट भारी॥

धन निर्धन को देत सदाहीं। जो कोई जांचे वो फल पाहीं॥
अस्तुति केहि विधि करौं तुम्हारी। क्षमहु नाथ अब चूक हमारी॥

शंकर हो संकट के नाशन। मंगल कारण विघ्न विनाशन॥
योगी यति मुनि ध्यान लगावैं। नारद शारद शीश नवावैं॥

नमो नमो जय नमः शिवाय। सुर ब्रह्मादिक पार न पाय॥
जो यह पाठ करे मन लाई। ता पर होत हैं शम्भु सहाई॥

ॠनियां जो कोई हो अधिकारी। पाठ करे सो पावन हारी॥
पुत्र हीन कर इच्छा कोई। निश्चय शिव प्रसाद तेहि होई॥

पण्डित त्रयोदशी को लावे। ध्यान पूर्वक होम करावे॥
त्रयोदशी ब्रत करे हमेशा। तन नहीं ताके रहे कलेशा॥

धूप दीप नैवेद्य चढ़ावे। शंकर सम्मुख पाठ सुनावे॥
जन्म जन्म के पाप नसावे। अन्तवास शिवपुर में पावे॥

Victory to the Lord of Parvati, merciful to the poor, who always protects the saints.
The crescent moon shines beautifully on your forehead, and you wear earrings of coiled snakes.

With a fair body and the Ganges flowing from your head, wearing a garland of skulls and smeared with holy ash.
Wearing tiger skin garments, your beautiful form enchants even sages and serpents.

Goddess Parvati, the beloved daughter of Maina, forms the beautiful left half of your body (Ardhanarishvara).
Holding a majestic trident in your hand, you continually destroy all enemies.

Nandi and Lord Ganesha look as beautiful beside you as lotuses in the middle of a lake.
Lord Kartikeya and Ganesha complete a divine picture that no one can fully describe.

Whenever the gods called out to you in distress, you immediately resolved their miseries.
When the demon Tarakasur caused terrible destruction, all the gods prayed to you.

You quickly sent the six-faced Kartikeya, who destroyed the demon in an instant.
You yourself killed the demon Jalandhar, and your glorious fame is known throughout the world.

You waged war against Tripurasur and mercifully saved everyone.
When King Bhagiratha performed severe penance, you fulfilled his vow (by holding the Ganges).

Among benefactors, there is no one equal to you. Your servants always praise you.
The Vedas sing the glory of your name, declaring you indescribable, eternal and boundless.

When deadly poison emerged during the churning of the ocean, terrifying both gods and demons,
You showed mercy and helped them by drinking it, thereby earning the name 'Neelakantha' (blue-throated).

Lord Rama worshiped you before winning Lanka and giving it to Vibhishana.
When Rama offered a thousand lotuses, you tested him by hiding one.

For the missing lotus, the lotus-eyed Rama prepared to offer his own eye.
Seeing such intense devotion, you were pleased and granted his desired boons.

Victory, victory, victory to the infinite and indestructible one! Dwelling in every heart, you bestow grace.
All wicked entities constantly trouble me, causing me to wander without peace.

I cry out "Save me, save me, O Lord!" I cannot accomplish my task on my own.
Take your trident and destroy my enemies. Come and rescue me from all crises.

Mother, father, brother, and others — no one helps when one is in distress.
You are my only hope, O Lord! Come and remove my heavy burdens.

You always grant wealth to the poor; whoever prays to you receives the fruit of their prayers.
How can I adequately praise you? Please forgive my mistakes, O Lord.

O Lord Shankar, you are the destroyer of distress, the cause of auspiciousness, and the remover of obstacles.
Yogis, ascetics, and sages meditate on you; Narada and Saraswati bow their heads to you.

Salutations to you, victory to 'Om Namah Shivaya'! Even Brahma and other gods cannot fathom your greatness.
Whoever recites this prayer with devotion, Lord Shiva always helps them.

If anyone is burdened by debt, reciting this prayer will make them debt-free.
If a childless person desires offspring, they will surely attain it by Shiva's grace.

Calling a priest on Trayodashi (the 13th lunar day) to perform a fire ritual with devotion,
And observing the Trayodashi fast always, ensures the body remains free from suffering.

Offering incense, lamps, and sacred food, and reading this Chalisa before Lord Shankar,
Destroys the sins of many lifetimes and secures residence in Shivapur (Shiva's abode) after death.

— ॥ —
दोहा — Doha

Lyrics in Hindi

नित्त नेम कर प्रातः ही, पाठ करौं चालीस।
तुम मेरी मनोकामना, पूर्ण करो जगदीश॥
मगसर छठि हेमन्त ॠतु, संवत चौसठ जान।
अस्तुति चालीसा शिवहि, पूर्ण कीन कल्याण॥

Making it a daily rule to recite this Chalisa every morning,
O Lord of the Universe, please fulfill all my desires.
In the season of Hemant, on the sixth day of Magsar month, in Samvat 64,
This Chalisa praising Lord Shiva was completed for the welfare of all.

About Shiv Chalisa: This Chalisa contains 40 devoted verses praising various acts of Lord Shiva, his appearance, and his mercy towards his devotees. Reciting it removes fears, grants wisdom, and brings peace and prosperity.
